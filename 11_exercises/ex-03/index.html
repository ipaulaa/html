Black Hole
A region in space where the gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape it.
Supernova
A massive explosion that occurs when a star exhausts its fuel and collapses under its own gravity, often leading to the creation of a neutron star or black hole.
Nebula
A large cloud of gas and dust in space, often a region where new stars are born or remnants of dead stars.
Planet
A celestial body that orbits a star, has sufficient mass to assume a nearly round shape, and has cleared its orbit of other debris.
Dwarf Planet
A celestial body that also orbits a star and has sufficient mass to be nearly round, but it has not cleared its orbital path of other debris.
Exoplanet
A planet that orbits a star outside our solar system.
